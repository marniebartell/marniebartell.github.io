Translated resources

Adding multilingual support

Summary

I was tasked with adding introductory and essential health information translated into various languages for audiences who are hard to reach normally .

Background

My role

Without translating the entire website, I was tasked with figuring out how to add non-English content for people who most often request interpreters in the health system.

Target users

  • Patients and families who don't speak English

Goal

Display emergency wait times and other essential health information, such as introductory videos and emergency department wait times, in different languages. This way we can reach isolated groups.

Supported languages

 

We support the following languages and translate introductory information, how to contact Health Link, healthcare options, and emergency wait times:

 

Base languages:

  • Arabic
  • Simplified Chinese
  • Traditional Chinese
  • French
  • Hindi
  • Punjabi
  • Somali
  • Spanish
  • Tigrinya
  • Vietnamese

Indigenous languages:

  • Alexis Stoney Nakota
  • Blackfoot
  • Cree
  • Dene
  • Stoney Nakoda

Additional supported languages

 

On occasion, we support additional languages for specific situations, such as workplace outbreaks and refugee support, including, but not limited to:

  • Amharic
  • Dari
  • Low German
  • Korean
  • Nepali
  • Oromo
  • Pashto
  • Portuguese
  • Tagalog
  • Thai
  • Ukrainian
  • Urdu

Results and outcomes

Learnings

  • I learned that we can provide information for hard-to-reach audiences in creative ways.
  • This allows our websites to be more equitable.
  • Even though the website traffic might be low, we are able to get essential information to audiences who are otherwise beyond reach.
